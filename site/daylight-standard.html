Daylight Equation Standard

Evidence-Bound Security Control Plane: Claim + Evidence + Provenance + Reproducibility + Boundary + Monitoring + Falsification = Authority.

What It Is

The Daylight Equation Standard defines how a security claim earns authority from evidence. The weakest required field governs the result.

  • NoEvidence(x) -> NoScore(x)
  • NoProvenance(x) -> NoAuthority(x)
  • NoExecution(x) -> NoRuntimeScore(x)
  • ManualScore(x) -> Reject(x)

What It Verifies

Claim

The statement, scope, allowed boundary, and forbidden expansions.

Evidence

Producer, commit, artifact digest, transcript digest, command, and outputs.

Authority

Release, install, trust, decrypt, publish, or deploy action allowed by policy.

Falsification

What would downgrade, block, or disprove the claim.

What It Does Not Verify

not production cryptography, not a general runtime sandbox, not post-quantum secure, not independently audited, not government endorsed, not cATO authorized, not RMF authorized, not FIPS validated, and not external certification.

Conformance Levels

Read levels D0-D9

D0 starts at research evidence. D9 requires actual external authority and cannot be self-issued.

Example Pass/Fail Claim

Pass: a standard-candidate claim linked to local schemas, docs, examples, and validation commands. Fail: a certification claim with no certification evidence.
